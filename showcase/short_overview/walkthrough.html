How φ, κ, δ, ρ relate to the region marginals  (click to expand)

Think of every car as belonging to a group (a region — say, flexible_local), and having its own personality within that group. The group sets the typical shape of arrival, dwell and SoC. The personality bends those typical shapes for this specific car.

The region shared by all cars in it
Sets the default shape for how this group of drivers behaves:
  • arrival time: when they show up (μ, σ)
  • dwell time: how long they stay (k, λ)
  • arrival SoC: how full the battery is when they arrive (α, β, shift)
  • ρ — whether early arrivers stay longer
The car's personality one draw per car
Three numbers, sampled once when the car is created, then stored in users.csv:
  • φ — how often they come at all
  • κ — how punctual they are
  • δ — how far their commute is (in km)
Each is a uniform draw between region-defined bounds.

Region presets in the panel above set the personality sliders to the middle of the region's bounds, and load the region's default shape. Drag any slider after that and the region selector flips to "custom" — meaning you've stepped outside any preset.

What each axis actually does

φ   frequency
Probability of showing up on any given weekday.
Bernoulli gate. Doesn't change how they arrive when they do — only whether.
Drag it: attendance bar moves; D5 panel gains "skipped" days. Histograms below don't change.
κ   consistency
How tight the arrival time is around the regional mean.
Shrinks the arrival spread: σ_eff = σ × (1 − κ × 0.5). High κ ⇒ tight; low κ ⇒ spread.
Drag it: arrival histogram narrows or widens around μ. Dwell and SoC don't move.
δ   commute (km)
One-way commute distance, used as a proxy for "how depleted" the arriving battery is.
Shifts the arrival-SoC distribution lower: shift_eff = shift_region − 0.003 × δ. Doesn't change its shape.
Drag it: SoC histogram slides left or right; its shape (skew, spread) stays the same.
ρ   copula correlation
Region-level (not personality). Couples arrival time with dwell length.
Gaussian copula. Negative ρ ⇒ "early arrivers stay longer". Doesn't change either marginal — only their joint behaviour.
Drag it: joint scatter tilts. Both arrival and dwell histograms stay statistically identical.
Key idea: the personality axes (φ, κ, δ) only ever bend the region's defaults at sample time — they never replace them. And ρ never touches the marginals at all: by construction, a copula couples without altering. That's how you can dial each axis independently and read off exactly which part of the picture it controls.

The per-car flow, in three steps

  1. Pick a region for the car (categorical draw over the 5 regions).
  2. Draw the car's personality (φ, κ, δ) from the region's bounds; record the region's defaults (μ, σ, k, λ, α, β, shift, ρ) as the car's fixed reference shape. Both go into users.csv conceptually.
  3. For every day in the sim window: φ decides if they show up. If yes, the copula at ρ draws a coupled (arrival, dwell); κ-shrunk σ shapes the arrival; δ-shifted Beta shapes the SoC. That row becomes one entry in sessions.csv.

Source: src/v2b_syndata/renderers/sessions.py (copula at line 44, sampling dispatch at line 124).

What the model is

Step 3 of sessions.csv is a bivariate Gaussian copula on (arrival_hour, dwell_hours):

z1, z2 ~ N(0,1)
z2' = ρ·z1 + √(1−ρ²)·z2
u1 = Φ(z1),  u2 = Φ(z2')
arrival = TruncNorm⁻¹(u1; μ, σ_eff, [lo, hi])
dwell   = Weibull⁻¹(u2; k, λ)

Why ρ matters: ρ < 0 ⇒ early arrivers stay longer. Implementation mirrors src/v2b_syndata/renderers/sessions.py:44.
