The data we need doesn't exist

PrivateReal charging logs identify drivers and buildings — they rarely leave the utility or campus that collected them.
Site-lockedThe public exceptions cover a handful of sites. You can't ask Caltech's dataset what Miami retail looks like — or what 500 EVs would do.
Missing the key variableNo charger ever records battery state of charge. The quantity every V2B optimizer schedules around is absent from every dataset.

So V2B methods get evaluated on data that is small, fixed, and blind to SoC.

the problem2

Our answer: generate it — under three guarantees

Physical

Building load is a real EnergyPlus simulation of DOE prototype buildings under real (TMYx) weather — not a shape template.

ASHRAE 90.1 prototypes · 15-min · 8 end-use meters

Calibrated

Driver behavior is fitted per-region from four real charging datasets, with fit quality reported and provenance stamped on every value.

ACN · EV WATTS · ElaadNL · INL — MLE + KS per region

Reproducible

A scenario file plus one integer seed regenerates the dataset byte-for-byte. Every run ships a manifest that can audit or re-create it.

(scenario, seed) → identical SHA-256s, forever

Every real driver becomes a point in a behavioral coordinate system — φ how often they plug in, κ how consistent their timing is, δ how far they drive. Regions of that plane are fitted separately, and a driver's region shapes everything they do.

The honest surprise we kept: real workplace drivers plug in rarely — 76% of ACN users sit at φ ≤ 0.3 (left boxes). The hand-authored grid would have missed this; the calibrated grid is the data.

claim 2 / 3 · calibrated7

…and real distributions replace textbook priors

■ synthetic prior: one morning peak · ■ fitted to ACN rare_consistent (n=4,723 sessions): bimodal — morning commute + after-lunch arrivals. Mixtures are kept only when they beat the single fit by KS ≥ 0.02.

  • Per region we fit: arrival (TruncNorm ± mixture), dwell (Weibull ± mixture), SoC (Betas), and an arrival↔dwell copula (ρ = −0.56 here: late arrivals stay shorter).
  • Every fitted value carries its provenance (calibration:acn_data_2019_2021) and KS fit quality into every dataset that uses it.
The honesty clause: no charger records SoC — anywhere. Our SoC quantities are modeled priors reconstructed from energy delivered ÷ inferred capacity. Calibrated to be consistent with ground truth; never claimed to be ground truth.

SoC reconstruction (the honesty clause in full): chargers meter energy, never battery state. Arrival SoC = 1 − (kWh delivered ÷ inferred battery capacity); capacity is inferred per user from their max observed session energy, falling back to class defaults for ~33% of ACN users. Departure requirement ∝ the energy the driver asked for. Both are priors made consistent with ground truth — and stamped as such.

Every sample is checked before it ships

Sessions are generated by rejection, not clamping — a candidate session must survive five physical gates (same-day, in-window, non-overlapping, SoC-increasing, energy-reachable within the dwell at the charger's rate). Then the whole dataset passes the invariant wall:

Scale and control: from one building to a campus

  • One config = one experiment. Swap the site (9 cities), building (6 DOE types), population (13 cohorts — 8 data-calibrated), chargers (V1G → full V2B), PV/battery — each swap is one line.
  • Monte-Carlo built in: months × samples batches where each sample gets its own weather realization and seed — a full 10-building campus × 12 months × 150 samples generated overnight.
  • Distribution-shift benchmarks for free: train on ACN-calibrated workplace, test on ElaadNL EU public or 2011-era INL residential.
