## Why

The committed ElaadNL TSTR baseline (`data/tstr/results_elaadnl_matched.json`)
trains on **one synthetic month from a 20-vehicle building** and tests on the
**1,231-driver, multi-year ElaadNL aggregate** (55,201 workplace-venue sessions,
Aug 2020–Oct 2024; 481 kW peak). A reviewer can attack the raw-regime gap as a
trivial scale mismatch rather than a fidelity statement. This study varies the
two mismatched axes independently and jointly:

| arm | months | fleet | command deltas (`tools/tstr_forecasting.py --real elaadnl --normalize`, seed 1234, scenario `S_elaadnl_public_eu`) |
|---|--:|---|---|
| baseline | 1 | 20 EVs / 20 chargers | (committed run, none) |
| A | 12 | 20 EVs / 20 chargers | `--months 12` |
| B | 1 | 1000 EVs / 500 chargers | `--override 'ev_fleet.ev_count=1000' --override 'charging_infra.charger_count=500'` |
| C (headline) | 12 | 1000 EVs / 500 chargers | both |

**Honest cap note:** the real cohort has **1,231 drivers**; `ev_fleet.ev_count`
is capped at **1000** and `charging_infra.charger_count` at **500** by the knob
registry (`configs/knobs.yaml`), so the "scale-matched" fleet sits ~19% below
the real driver count. We state this rather than lifting the caps.

`--months N` generates N **consecutive calendar months** (knob override
`sim_window.start` advanced one month at a time from the scenario anchor,
2020-04 → 2021-03) with the **same fleet**: user/car latents are seeded by node
name + car_id, while per-day session draws are keyed by the absolute date, so
each month is a distinct realization of the same cohort (a longitudinal series,
not twelve copies of one month; arm A monthly session counts 75–117 confirm
this).

## Reading

1. **Shape parity is already there and is scale/duration-invariant.** The
   calendar-only normalized probe — the regime that isolates what the generator's
   load *shape* contributes, with no persistence and no magnitude — is at
   **1.01–1.03× TRTR in every arm**. Neither shrinking the fleet 50× nor cutting
   the training window 12× moves it. This is the strongest counter to the scale
   attack: the fidelity claim never depended on scale.
2. **Most of the raw lagged gap is indeed scale.** Matching the fleet (arm B)
   alone cuts the raw lagged ratio from 7.38× to 3.05× MAE. So the reviewer is
   right that the raw baseline number is scale-dominated — and the fix confirms
   rather than undermines the utility claim.
3. **The residual raw gap is activity rate, not fleet size.** Driver-count
   matching *overshoots* magnitude: 1000 synthetic workplace EVs produce a
   196 kW mean vs the real 33 kW (peak 998 vs 481). The real cohort charges on
   a public network sporadically (55,201 sessions / 1,231 drivers / ~51 months
   ≈ 0.9 sessions·driver⁻¹·mo⁻¹) whereas the calibrated workplace cohort
   charges near-daily (≈ 5.4 sessions·EV⁻¹·mo⁻¹, ×6.1 — matching the ×5.9 mean
   overshoot). Raw calendar ratios rise in B/C for the same reason (magnitude
   offset with no lag features to compensate). Matching *load* rather than
   *headcount* would need ev_count ≈ 170–200; the honest statement is that no
   single fleet size can match both the driver count and the per-driver
   activity of a public-network cohort with a workplace-calibrated population.
4. **More training months help the operational forecaster.** Same-fleet
   12-month training (arm A) improves lagged-normalized transfer 4.19× → 3.01×
   MAE (and slightly improves every other regime): with 8,748 training bins the
   model sees seasonal/holiday variation a single month cannot supply.
5. **Why lagged-normalized worsens at 1000 EVs (5.25–5.55×):** a 1000-EV
   aggregate is far *smoother* relative to its mean than the real series (which
   carries multi-year nonstationarity: COVID-era ramp, fleet growth, holidays).
   After unit-mean scaling, the synthetic lag features live in a
   low-relative-variance regime the real test tail never visits. This is a
   genuine (and now quantified) limitation of a stationary single-building
   generator against a 4-year evolving network — not a scale artifact.

Headline for the paper: **arm C** (12 synthetic months, knob-capped
scale-matched fleet) with the calendar-normalized 1.01–1.02× parity and the
raw-lagged 7.4×→4.3× (B: 3.05×) scale decomposition.

## E5 / validation notes

- **No E5 infeasibility in any arm.** Arm B/C realized max concurrency
  229–246 of 500 chargers (headroom ≈ 2×); arm A 5–9 of 20.
- **Zero hard validation errors** in all 25 generated months. Every month
  carries the same 2 soft warnings, both benign and pre-existing on this
  scenario: `G5b` (region 'erratic' present in axes_distribution but not
  calibrated for this population) and `S2` (informational: held-out KS deferred
  to calibration Step 5.5).
