## Contention design

- The uncontended unit's realized peak concurrency is **59** (its manifest E5 block; mean work-hour concurrency ~42). `charging_infra.charger_count = 35` ≈ 60% of that observed peak, so plug contention binds hard on every workday while most sessions remain servable.
- The unit deliberately violates the generator's E5 physical-realism invariant (18.1% of ticks have more active sessions than chargers; the manifest records the validation error). That is the point: offered demand exceeds the plugs, and the DISPATCH layer — not the generator — must resolve it. The unit is a benchmark stress input, not part of the released corpus.
- Plug scarcity is resolved by the bench adapter's FCFS admission (`bench/adapter.py`): a session that arrives with no free charger is rejected and never plugs in. Admission is a pool POLICY, identical for every algorithm — every arm below sees the same admitted set. **Mean queueing delay is not reported because the machinery models plug-on-arrival-or-reject; no session waits.**
- The scheduling algorithms contend over the FEEDER cap: 87.5 kW = 0.125 x 35 chargers x 20 kW. The 0.125 service ratio is the ACN-Caltech-like operating point documented in `bench/adapter.py`. At an unbinding feeder (ratio 1.0) all six controlled schedulers coincide exactly even on this plug-scarce unit — admitted sessions never share a constraint — so the feeder cap is what makes queue ORDER matter.

## LP arms (labeled relaxations)

The LP's static session→charger assignment cannot express plug contention — a faithful assignment model needs big-M / integer machinery, deliberately NOT attempted here. Instead the LP serves ALL sessions (no admission) under an aggregate EV-power cap `sum_s (c + d) <= cap` (discharge counts: a plug carries |power|; the stationary battery is behind the meter and exempt):

- `*_lp_plugcap` — cap = chargers x rate = 700 kW: the honest fractional-plug-sharing proxy for the plug pool.
- `v1g_lp_feedercap` — cap = the acnsim arms' feeder cap (87.5 kW): same power envelope as the schedulers, but load-aware and admission-free.

These rows answer "what could a building-load-aware optimizer do with the same total plug power if plug-swapping were frictionless" — an upper bound on scheduling value, not a plug-level simulation.

## Reading the table

- `uncontrolled_nolimit` reproduces the uncontended table's uncontrolled arm (same demand; infrastructure limits ignored) — the common baseline for peak reduction.
- `acnsim_uncontrolled` ignores the feeder cap by design (cap-violating upper bound) and over-delivers past the requested energy by ACN-Sim's own semantics.
- The six controlled schedulers all draw exactly the feeder cap during the rush, so their PEAKS nearly coincide; they separate on ENERGY SERVICE — deadline-aware orderings (EDF/LLF) satisfy every admitted session, while deadline-blind orderings (FCFS/LCFS/LRPT/RR) strand energy in sessions that depart unserved.
- The LP rows serve every session (no admission) AND shave the building peak — locating the two distinct sources of dispatch value: queue/admission management under contention (scheduler-side) vs building-load-aware timing (optimizer-side).
